About Blood

Blood is essential for life. Donating blood saves lives and helps people in critical need. Every donation can help save the lives of patients undergoing surgery, accident victims, and those with chronic illnesses like cancer.

Donating blood is a simple and safe process that is crucial to maintaining a steady supply of blood. It is safe, easy, and life-saving!

Understanding Blood Groups

Blood is classified into four main groups based on the presence or absence of certain antigens. These are:

  • Blood Group A: Has A antigens on red blood cells and anti-B antibodies in plasma. Can donate blood to A and AB groups and can receive blood from A and O types.
  • Blood Group B: Has B antigens on red blood cells and anti-A antibodies in plasma. Can donate blood to B and AB groups and can receive blood from B and O types.
  • Blood Group AB: Has both A and B antigens on red blood cells and no anti-A or anti-B antibodies. This group is the universal plasma donor and can receive blood from A, B, AB, and O types.
  • Blood Group O: Has no A or B antigens on red blood cells but has both anti-A and anti-B antibodies. This group is the universal red blood cell donor and can donate blood to A, B, AB, and O types, but can only receive blood from O type donors.
